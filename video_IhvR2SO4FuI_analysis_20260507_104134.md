Based on the video, Shamba Connect is an organization founded and led by CEO Mercy Munene, dedicated to promoting health through organic, small-space gardening.

### **Key Services**
*   **Garden Installation:** They design and install "attractive gardens" for both private homes and public institutions.
*   **Livestock Management:** The organization keeps and manages rabbits and goats.
*   **End-to-End Support:** Beyond just providing farm inputs and planting, they offer training for their clients to ensure the gardens are sustainable.
*   **Digital Integration:** They utilize mobile technology for business operations, including receiving payments via till numbers, performing reversals, and conducting online research to improve their services.

### **Mission and Impact**
The mission of Shamba Connect is rooted in providing access to organic, pesticide-free vegetables to combat health issues like acidity and malnutrition. A core part of their mission is social impact through **Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)** projects. 
*   **Example:** At the Makongeni Health Centre, they installed kitchen gardens to help pregnant women struggling with low hemoglobin (HB) levels. By providing direct access to leafy greens, they helped improve patients' HB levels from 8 to a healthy 11.

### **Team and Collective Identity**
Shamba Connect describes itself as a **proactive and supportive collective**. 
*   **"Going Beyond":** The team emphasizes that they do not just sell products; they "go beyond" by becoming partners in their clients' health journeys through training and ongoing support.
*   **Collaborative Language:** Throughout the video, the work is described using "we" (e.g., "we train clients," "we support them," "we came up with a solution"), highlighting a unified team effort. 
*   **Community Focused:** They view themselves as a "preferred center" for organic knowledge, aiming to be the primary resource for anyone interested in small-space gardening and healthy living.
